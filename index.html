cat

The cat (Felis catus), commonly referred to as the domestic cat or house cat, is a small domesticated carnivorous mammal. It is the only domesticated species of the family Felidae. Recent advances in archaeology and genetics have shown that the domestication of the cat occurred in the Near East around 7500 BC.

Thai cat

Thai cats are cats that originated in Thailand. The characteristic that makes Thai cats superior to other types of cats is their personality. Thai cats are intelligent, independent, know how to think, know how to flatter, love their home, love their owners, and above all, love their own freedom with all their hearts and minds. They are free to eat, drink, or go wherever they want, which is considered their own personality that makes them different from other breeds of cats. The colors of Thai cats are one factor that makes cat lovers feel happy when they look at them, whether they are Siamese cats, nine-pointed cats, Khao Manee or pure white cats, Nilrat or pure black cats, Suphalak or copper cats, Si Sawat cats, or Korat Thai cats, all of them have received attention from owners and those interested.



Persian

Persian is a cat that originated in Persia or Iran. It has been raised in various countries in Europe and America for almost a hundred years. For Thailand, it is considered the first foreign cat breed that was introduced because it is a cat with a gentle, calm, sociable nature. It is cheerful, mischievous, likes to flatter and is quick-witted. In addition, there is a reference to the origin of Persian cats that has been around since 1,684 BC. The first Persian cat ancestor that has been recorded as evidence was imported to Europe in 1620. It was a gray cat from Khorasan, Persia. This one went to Italy. And a white long-haired cat from Angora (now Ankara), Turkey went to France.
